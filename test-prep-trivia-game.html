Round 3 - Answer 1
Question: What is the comparative form of "big"?
Answer:

Explanation: For short adjectives (1 syllable), add "-er". Double the final consonant if it ends in a consonant-vowel-consonant pattern.
‚úÖ "big" ‚Üí "bigger" | "hot" ‚Üí "hotter" | "fast" ‚Üí "faster"

Question: What is the comparative form of "beautiful"?
Answer:

Explanation: For long adjectives (2+ syllables), use "more" + adjective.
‚úÖ "beautiful" ‚Üí "more beautiful"
‚úÖ "interesting" ‚Üí "more interesting"
‚úÖ "difficult" ‚Üí "more difficult"

Example Answers:

Explanation: Many answers are possible! Just remember: short adjectives (1 syllable) use "-er", long adjectives (2+ syllables) use "more".
‚úÖ "Coffee is stronger than tea" (strong = 1 syllable ‚Üí stronger)
‚úÖ "Coffee is more popular than tea" (popular = 3 syllables ‚Üí more popular)

Question: Fill in the blank: I _____ (study) English every day.
Answer:
study
Explanation: "Every day" shows present simple tense. With "I", use the base form V1 (study).
‚úÖ "I study English every day" (present simple - habit/routine)
‚úÖ "I study" | "You study" | "We study" | "They study"

Question: Correct the sentence: "She doesn't eats pizza."
Answer:
She doesn't eat pizza.
Explanation: After "doesn't" (or "don't/didn't"), you ALWAYS use the base form V1, never V1-3rd or V2.
‚úÖ "She doesn't eat pizza" (base form after doesn't)
‚ùå Wrong: "She doesn't eats" | "She doesn't ate"

Question: Fill in the blank: I _____ (not go) to school yesterday because I was sick.

Explanation: "Yesterday" shows past tense. Negative past uses "didn't" + base form V1 (go).
‚úÖ "I didn't go to school yesterday"
‚ùå Wrong: "I didn't went" (never use V2 after didn't!)

Answer:

Explanation: Present continuous structure: Subject + am/is/are + V1-ing + object + time.
‚úÖ "I am studying English now" (present continuous - happening now)
‚úÖ Pattern: I am + V1-ing | She is + V1-ing | They are + V1-ing

Round 5 - Answer 1
Question: When do you use present continuous vs future continuous for future events?
Answer:
Use present continuous for planned/arranged future events. Use future continuous for actions that will be in progress.
Explanation: Both can talk about the future, but: ‚Ä¢ Present continuous = planned/arranged events (I'm meeting her tomorrow) ‚Ä¢ Future continuous = actions in progress at a future time (I'll be studying at 3pm)
‚úÖ "I am meeting my friend tomorrow" (present continuous - planned)
‚úÖ "I will be studying at 3pm tomorrow" (future continuous - in progress)
Round 5 - Answer 2
Question: What tense is the interrupter verb in when you're using past continuous? For example, in "I was cooking when she arrived."
Answer:
Past Simple (V2)
Explanation: When you use past continuous (was/were + V1-ing) for the longer/ongoing action, the interrupting action (the one that stops it) is always in Past Simple (V2).
‚úÖ "I was cooking when she arrived" (past continuous + past simple)
‚úÖ "They were playing when it started to rain" (past continuous + past simple)
‚úÖ Pattern: was/were + V1-ing (ongoing) + when/while + V2 (interruption)
‚ùå Wrong: "I was cooking when she was arriving" (both can't be continuous!)
Round 5 - Answer 3
Question: When do we need to add a determiner before an object?
Answer:
We need determiners for singular objects. Plural or uncountable (numberless) objects don't need determiners.
Explanation: This is a critical rule! Singular = one specific thing. Plural/uncountable = more than one or not countable.
‚úÖ "I read a book" (singular - needs determiner: a/an/the/my/your)
‚úÖ "I eat pizza" (plural/uncountable - no determiner)
‚úÖ "I drink coffee" (uncountable - no determiner)
‚ùå "I read book" (singular missing determiner)
Round 5 - Answer 4
Question: Where do adverbs of frequency (like "usually", "often", "always") go in a sentence? Give an example.
Answer:
Adverbs of frequency go before the main verb, but after the verb "be" (am/is/are/was/were).
Explanation: Placement rules: ‚Ä¢ Before main verbs: "I usually study" | "She often goes"
‚Ä¢ After "be" verbs: "I am always tired" | "He is usually happy"
‚Ä¢ With "do/does/did": Adverb goes after: "I don't usually eat"
‚úÖ "I usually study in the morning" (before main verb)
‚úÖ "She is always on time" (after "be" verb)
‚úÖ "They often play soccer" (before main verb)
‚úÖ "I don't usually watch TV" (after don't/doesn't/didn't)
