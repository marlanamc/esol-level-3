# Sentence Builder Cards - Teacher Guide

## Quick Reference: Card Categories

| Color | Category | What's Included |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Light Blue | **Pronouns** | I, You, He, She, They, We |
| Teal | **To Be** | am, is, are, was, were, am not, is not, are not, was not, were not |
| Mint | **Helper Verbs** | do, does, did, will, do not, does not, did not, will not |
| Lime Green | **V1/V1-3rd** | Base verbs (eat/eats, work/works, etc.) |
| Sage Green | **V1-ing** | Verb-ing forms (eating, working, etc.) |
| Mint Green | **V2** | Past simple verbs (ate, worked, etc.) |
| Peach | **Objects** | pizza, coffee, book, homework, etc. |
| Coral Pink | **Time** | now, today, yesterday, tomorrow, etc. |
| Periwinkle | **Places** | home, school, work, park |
| Pink | **Prepositions** | at, in, on, to, from, with |
| Apricot | **Determiners** | a, an, the, my, your |
| Purple | **Connectors** | when, while, before, after, as, until |

## Before the Lesson

### Preparation
1. **Print & Cut Cards**: Print cards double-sided, cut them out (2.2" Ã— 1.4")
2. **Storage**: Organize by color/category in small boxes, envelopes, or baggies
3. **Print Scaffolding Guide**: One copy per student or table group
4. **Optional**: Laminate cards for durability

### First-Time Setup (15-20 minutes)
- Introduce card categories one at a time
- Show students how to match colors on front/back
- Demonstrate building ONE simple sentence together
- Explain the color-coding system

## Lesson Structure Suggestions

### Introduction (10-15 min)
1. **Show cards**: Display each category, explain the colors
2. **Build together**: Create one sentence as a class (e.g., "I eat pizza")
3. **Explain the guide**: Show students how to follow the scaffolding levels
4. **Practice rules**: Cards stay on the table, read from left to right

### Main Activity (30-45 min)
- Students work through scaffolding guide levels 1-3 (or assigned level)
- Walk around and check sentences
- Encourage self-correction using guide examples

### Closing (5-10 min)
- Share 2-3 student sentences with class
- Quick review of what they learned

## Common Student Mistakes & How to Help

### 1. **Wrong verb form with He/She/It**
   - **Mistake**: "He eat pizza" 
   - **Fix**: Show them V1 cards say "eat/eats" - need "eats" for He/She/It
   - **Tip**: Have them point to the subject card first, then choose verb form

### 2. **Missing determiners with singular objects**
   - **Mistake**: "She prepares report" (missing "a")
   - **Fix**: Refer to Level 1 tip box - singular objects need determiners
   - **Tip**: Create a visual rule: "If object is ONE thing â†’ need determiner"

### 3. **Wrong verb after "do not"**
   - **Mistake**: "I do not eats" or "She does not prepares"
   - **Fix**: Show them after "do not/does not" ALWAYS use base form (V1)
   - **Tip**: Point to Level 10 (Negative Statements) - rule is clearly stated

### 4. **Confusing V1-ing vs V1 vs V2**
   - **Mistake**: Using "eating" in simple present, or "eat" in past continuous
   - **Fix**: Use color memory - all verbs are green shades but different:
     - Lime green = V1 (simple present)
     - Sage green = V1-ing (continuous)
     - Mint green = V2 (past simple)
   - **Tip**: Ask "Is this happening now?" â†’ use V1-ing

### 5. **Placing cards in wrong order**
   - **Mistake**: "do not" before subject, or object before verb
   - **Fix**: Have them read scaffolding guide formula: Subject â†’ Verb â†’ Object â†’ Time
   - **Tip**: Practice reading cards left-to-right as a sentence

### 6. **Missing connector in past continuous**
   - **Mistake**: "I was cooking" (no second event/time)
   - **Fix**: Refer to Level 6 - past continuous needs "when" or time word
   - **Tip**: Past continuous tells a story - what was happening WHEN something else happened

## How to Check Student Work

### Quick Visual Check:
1. **Color pattern**: Does it match the formula colors in the guide?
2. **Left-to-right**: Subject â†’ Verb â†’ Object? (basic sentence)
3. **Card count**: Too many cards = probably wrong structure

### Question Prompts:
- "Can you read this sentence to me?"
- "What color cards did you use? Do they match the guide?"
- "Look at Level X - does your sentence match that formula?"

### Common Corrections:
- If sentence is wrong, don't fix it for them
- Point to the correct level in guide: "Check Level X formula"
- Ask: "What does the guide say comes after the subject?"

## Differentiation Ideas

### For Struggling Students:
- Start with Level 1 only (Pronouns + V1 + Object)
- Provide sentence frames: "I ___ ___" (blank cards)
- Work in pairs - one student reads guide, other builds
- Give them ONLY the cards they need (pre-select by category)

### For Advanced Students:
- Skip to Level 5 (Present Continuous - Full Sentences)
- Challenge: Build negative sentences
- Challenge: Use connectors to create longer sentences
- Extension: Write their sentences, then trade with partner

### Mixed Levels:
- Set up "stations" by level
- Students move through levels at their own pace
- Provide answer cards for self-checking

## Troubleshooting

### "I can't find the right card!"
- **Solution**: Organize cards by color before starting
- **Solution**: Check if they're looking at the right category
- **Tip**: Show card backs to help identify category

### "I don't understand the formula"
- **Solution**: Build one sentence together step-by-step
- **Solution**: Use physical demonstration: "This card is Subject, this is Verb..."
- **Tip**: Have them point to each card while reading guide formula

### "All my sentences look the same"
- **Solution**: Encourage them to use different pronouns
- **Solution**: Mix up objects and time words
- **Challenge**: "Can you make 5 different sentences with 'I'?"

### Cards getting messy/lost
- **Prevention**: Use trays or designated table spaces
- **Prevention**: Card limits: "Use only 10 cards at a time"
- **Organization**: Sort cards back by color when done

## Extension Activities

1. **Sentence Race**: "Who can build a sentence first using Level 2 formula?"
2. **Story Building**: Build 3-4 sentences that tell a story
3. **Card Trading**: Students write sentences, trade with partner who rebuilds
4. **Time Challenge**: "Build 5 sentences in 10 minutes"
5. **Sentence Detective**: Teacher builds wrong sentence, students find the mistake

## Assessment Ideas

- **Observation**: Watch how students use cards - do they follow the guide?
- **Quick Check**: Collect 2-3 sentences per student, photograph or have them write
- **Peer Check**: Students check each other's sentences using the guide
- **Exit Ticket**: "Write one sentence you built today" (use card words)

## Time Management Tips

- **First lesson**: Focus on Levels 1-3 only (45 min total)
- **Subsequent lessons**: Add one new level each session
- **Review**: Spend 5 min reviewing previous level before new one
- **Practice**: 20-25 min building, 10 min sharing/checking

## Common Concerns & Solutions

### "What if students just copy the guide examples?"
- Guide examples use different words than cards (designed this way!)
- Encourage creativity: "Make YOUR own sentence, not the example"
- Check that their sentences are different from guide

### "What if students mix up tenses?"
- This is normal! Guide is organized by tense for a reason
- Start with one tense (simple present) until mastered
- Use color memory: continuous tenses need teal "To Be" cards

### "What if it's too easy/hard for some?"
- That's why there are 10 levels! Let students find their level
- Pair stronger with weaker students for peer teaching
- Provide extension challenges for advanced students
