Understanding Interviews

How Interviews Work

Interviews are conversations. The goal is not perfection. The goal is clear communication.

  • Listen carefully to the question
  • Pause and think before answering
  • Speak in complete sentences
  • Keep your answer simple and clear

Remember

It's okay to make mistakes. You can say "Let me think" or "Can you repeat the question?"

Shake hands. Make eye contact. You are ready.

You Have

Experience. Skills. You are ready.

Building Strong Answers

How to Structure Your Answer

A strong answer usually has 2–3 parts:

1 A clear statement
2 A short example from your experience
3 What you learned (optional)

Tip

Interviewers ask the same questions in different words. Use the same answer structure!

Example

"I am organized. In my last job, I managed schedules for my team. This helped us work more efficiently."

Slide 5 of 17
Question 1

Tell Me About Yourself

Simple Structure

"I currently work as ____."

"I have experience in ____."

"I am interested in this position because ____."

Example

"I currently work as a housekeeper and team lead. I have experience in cleaning rooms and supporting my team. I am interested in this position because I enjoy helping customers and working with a team."

What are they looking for?

  • A short summary of your work experience
  • Why you are interested in this job
  • That you can speak clearly
They do not want your life story
Your Turn

Tell me about yourself. Use your real experience!

Slide 6 of 17
Question 2

What Experience Do You Have?

Simple Structure

"I worked as ____ for ____ years."

"In that job, I ____."

"This experience taught me ____."

Example

"I worked as a janitor for two years, then as a housekeeper. In that job, I led a small team and kept things organized. This experience taught me to work efficiently and pay close attention to detail."

What are they looking for?

  • Jobs or skills that match this position
  • Specific examples of what you did
Tip: Even if your past job was different, find skills that connect
Your Turn

What experience do you have? Share one or two examples.

Slide 7 of 17
Question 3

What Are Your Strengths?

Simple Structure

"One of my strengths is ____."

"For example, in my last job, I ____."

Example

"One of my strengths is being hardworking and a supportive leader. For example, in my last job as a housekeeper, I finished my rooms on time and helped train new staff."

What are they looking for?

  • Skills that will help you do this job well
  • A real example, not just a word
Good strengths: communication, organization, teamwork, patience
Your Turn

What is one of your strengths? Give an example.

Slide 8 of 17
Question 4

What Is One Weakness You Are Improving?

Simple Structure

"In the past, I sometimes ____."

"I realized this was a problem because ____."

"Now I am improving by ____."

Example

"In the past, I sometimes worked too fast and made small mistakes. I realized this was a problem because details are important. Now I am improving by slowing down and double-checking my work before I finish."

What are they looking for?

  • That you are honest and working to get better
  • A "safe" weakness
Safe weaknesses: public speaking, asking for help, being too detailed, speaking up in meetings.

Important

Choose a weakness that shows growth and improvement.

Then explain what you do now to improve.

Your Turn

What is one weakness you are working on?

Slide 9 of 17
Question 4 Support

Weakness Answers to Avoid

Avoid answers that make you sound unsafe, unreliable, or hard to work with.

Do Not Say

  • "I am always late."
  • "I don't like working with people."
  • "I get angry easily."
  • "I don't follow instructions."
Better Example

"In the past, I sometimes struggled to speak in meetings. I realized this was a problem because I had ideas to share. Now I am improving by preparing notes and practicing before I speak."

Quick Practice

Say one safe weakness and one improvement step.

Slide 13 of 17
Weakness Practice

Safe Weakness Formula

Use this 3-part formula so your answer sounds honest and professional.

1 Past challenge: "In the past, I sometimes ____."
2 Action now: "Now I ____ to improve."
3 Result: "This helps me ____."
Model Answer

"In the past, I was quiet in team meetings. Now I prepare one point before each meeting and share it to improve. This helps me communicate more clearly with my team."

Speak It

Choose one safe weakness and complete all 3 parts.

Slide 10 of 17
Question 5

How Do You Handle an Unhappy Customer?

1 "First, I stay calm."
2 "Then, I listen to understand the problem."
3 "Finally, I try to find a solution."
Example

"A customer was upset because something was not correct. First, I stayed calm. Then, I listened to understand the problem. Finally, I found a solution and fixed it right away. The customer felt better."

What are they looking for?

  • That you stay calm
  • That you can solve problems
  • That you are professional
Your Turn

How do you handle a difficult situation? Share an example if you can.

Question 6

Why Do You Want This Job?

Simple Structure

"I want this job because ____."

"I have experience in ____."

"I am ready to ____."

Example

"I want this job because I like working with people. I have experience in hospitality. I am ready to learn and grow."

What are they looking for?

  • That you understand the job
  • That you are motivated
  • A clear, honest reason

Bonus Questions

You Might Also Be Asked

If they ask these, use the same structure: short answer + example.

Can you describe a time you solved a problem at work?

"There was a problem. I ____. The result was ____."

What makes you a good fit for this job?

Combine: your experience + your strengths + why you want the job.

How do you stay organized during a busy shift?

"I make a list. I do ____ first. I stay calm and focus."

What is your availability?

"I am available ____. I can be flexible when needed."

Slide 14 of 17
Interview Language

Reframe Risky Answers

Say the same truth in a professional way.

Avoid This

  • "I want more money."
  • "My last boss was bad."
  • "I just need any job."
  • "I hate dealing with difficult people."

Try This Instead

  • "I am looking for more responsibility and growth."
  • "I learned a lot there, and now I want a better fit."
  • "I am excited for a role where I can contribute long-term."
  • "I stay calm, listen, and focus on solutions."
Reframe Drill

Pick one risky sentence and say a professional version.

Slide 15 of 17
Interview Language

No Direct Experience? Use This Reframe

Use this frame: "I have not worked as a ____ yet, but in my ____ role I ____, and that helps me ____."

Plug-and-Play Examples (1-4)

  • "I have not worked front desk yet, but I speak with customers every day, and that helps me communicate clearly."
  • "I have not been a supervisor yet, but I helped train new coworkers, and that helps me lead a team."
  • "I have not worked in a hotel yet, but I keep work areas organized, and that helps me maintain standards."
  • "I have not handled check-ins yet, but I use scheduling systems, and that helps me manage guest flow."

Plug-and-Play Examples (5-8)

  • "I have not worked concierge yet, but I solve customer questions quickly, and that helps me support guests."
  • "I have not worked night shift yet, but I am reliable and punctual, and that helps me cover important hours."
  • "I have not managed complaints as a supervisor yet, but I stay calm with upset people, and that helps me resolve problems."
  • "I have not worked luxury hospitality yet, but I focus on details and professionalism, and that helps me provide high-quality service."
Your Turn

Pick one target role and build one honest transfer-skills answer.

Job Posting Skills

How to Read and Decode a Job Posting

When you read a job ad, look for these 4 parts first.

1 Job title: What role is this?
2 Responsibilities: What tasks will you do every day?
3 Required skills: What must you already do well?
4 Schedule/availability: When do they need you to work?

From This Hotel Posting

  • Title: Hotel Supervisor
  • Tasks: supervise staff, solve guest issues, support front desk
  • Skills: communication, leadership, organization
  • Schedule: full-time, weekend and holiday availability
Quick Check

Can you find these 4 parts in the posting with your partner?

Job Posting Skills

Translate Job Words Into Your Answer

Use this sentence frame: "The posting says ____. In my experience, I ____."

Job Posting Says...

  • "Resolve guest complaints professionally"
  • "Lead and train team members"
  • "Stay organized during busy shifts"

Slide 22 of 17
American Job Culture

Talking About Salary Professionally

It is okay to discuss pay. In U.S. interviews, focus on fit first, then discuss compensation clearly and respectfully.

Professional Phrases

  • "Could you share the salary range for this role?"
  • "Based on my experience, I am targeting ____."
  • "I am interested in growth and long-term contribution."
  • "I am open to discussing total compensation."
Reframe

Instead of: "I want more money."

Try: "I am looking for a role with more responsibility, growth, and compensation that matches my experience."

Practice

Ask about salary range, then give your target in one clear sentence.
