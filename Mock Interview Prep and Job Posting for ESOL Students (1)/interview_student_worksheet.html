Interview Practice — Student Worksheet

Complete written responses using the sentence frames below, then practice aloud with a partner. Aim for clear, complete sentences and a steady pace.

Part 1. Opening

Say: "Hi, my name is ______________. I am here for the ______________ (job title) interview."

Part 2. Core Interview Questions — Sentence Structures

Q1: Tell me about yourself.

  • I currently work as ____.
  • I have experience in ____.
  • I am interested in this position because ____.

Q2: What experience do you have?

  • I worked as ____ for ____ years/months.
  • In that job, I ____.
  • This experience taught me ____.

Q3: What are your strengths?

  • One of my strengths is ____.
  • For example, I ____.

Q4: What is one weakness you are improving?

  • In the past, I sometimes ____.
  • Now I ____ to improve.
  • This helps me ____.

Part 5. No Direct Experience? Use This Reframe

Sentence frame: "I have not worked as a ____ yet, but in my ____ role I ____, and that helps me ____."
Target role/task Reframe example
Front desk / check-in "I have not worked front desk yet, but I speak with customers every day, and that helps me communicate clearly."
Team leadership "I have not been a supervisor yet, but I helped train new coworkers, and that helps me support a team."
Hotel experience "I have not worked in a hotel yet, but I keep work areas organized, and that helps me maintain high standards."
Guest complaints "I have not handled complaints as a supervisor yet, but I stay calm with upset people, and that helps me solve problems."

Part 6. Final Questions

Q5: How do you handle an unhappy guest?

  • First, I ____.
  • Then, I ____.
  • Finally, I ____.

Q6: Why do you want this job?

  • I want this job because ____.
  • I have experience in ____.
  • I am ready to ____.

Q7: Why did you leave your last job?

  • I learned a lot in that role, especially ____.
  • Now I am looking for ____.
  • This position is a good fit because ____.

Q8: How do you handle conflict with a coworker?

  • First, I ____.
  • Then, I ____.
  • My goal is to ____.

Q9: What does good customer service mean to you?

  • Good customer service means ____.
  • For example, I ____.
  • This helps customers/guests ____.

Bonus: What is your availability?

  • I am available ____.
  • I can be flexible when needed.

Bonus: If they ask your salary range

  • I am open to discussing pay based on the role and the full package.
  • My expectation is in the ____ to ____ range, depending on responsibilities.
  • I am flexible and want to find a fit that works for both of us.

Speaking checklist: Maintain eye contact; speak in a clear voice; use complete sentences; maintain a calm, steady pace.
