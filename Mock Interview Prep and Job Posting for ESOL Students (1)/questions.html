A

Say: "Hi, I'm ____. I am the Front Desk Manager. I will be interviewing you."

  • 1. Tell me about yourself.
  • 2. Why did you leave your last job?
  • 3. What does good customer service mean to you?
  • 4. Tell me about a time you handled an unhappy guest.
  • 5. How do you handle conflict with a coworker during a busy shift?
  • 6. What is your availability, including weekends and holidays?

Candidate asks manager: "What are the biggest front desk challenges in the first 90 days?"

B

Say: "Hi, I'm ____. I am the Housekeeping Supervisor. I will be interviewing you."

  • 1. Tell me about yourself.
  • 2. What housekeeping or quality-control experience do you have?
  • 3. What is one weakness you are improving, and what are you doing now?
  • 4. How do you keep quality high when rooms must be finished quickly?
  • 5. How do you coach a team member who is missing standards?
  • 6. Why did you leave your last job?

Candidate asks manager: "How is room quality measured here, and what does success look like?"

C

Say: "Hi, I'm ____. I am the Restaurant Manager. I will be interviewing you."

  • 1. Tell me about yourself.
  • 2. What experience do you have in hospitality or customer service?
  • 3. Describe a time you solved a guest problem quickly.
  • 4. What does good customer service mean to you?
  • 5. How do you handle conflict with a coworker during service?
  • 6. Why are you interested in this position at this hotel?

Candidate asks manager: "How do departments work together when a guest has a food-service complaint?"

D

Say: "Hi, I'm ____. I am the Human Resources Coordinator. I will be interviewing you."

  • 1. Tell me about yourself.
  • 2. What previous jobs have prepared you for this leadership role?
  • 3. What is one professional strength you are proud of? Give an example.
  • 4. What is one weakness you are currently improving?
  • 5. Why did you leave your last job?
  • 6. What salary range are you targeting for this role?

Candidate asks manager: "What training and growth opportunities are available for supervisors?"

E

Say: "Hi, I'm ____. I am the Guest Services Manager. I will be interviewing you."

  • 1. Tell me about yourself.
  • 2. What experience do you have leading or supporting a team?
  • 3. How do you handle a difficult customer or guest?
  • 4. Tell me about a time you turned a bad guest experience into a good one.
  • 5. How do you stay professional when someone is upset with you?
  • 6. What makes you a good fit for this job?

Candidate asks manager: "What guest-service skills are most important in this role?"

F

Say: "Hi, I'm ____. I am the Operations Manager. I will be interviewing you."

  • 1. Tell me about yourself.
  • 2. What responsibilities did you have in your last job?
  • 3. How do you stay organized during a very busy shift?
  • 4. Describe a time you had multiple urgent tasks. How did you prioritize?
  • 5. How do you handle conflict with a coworker while meeting deadlines?
  • 6. Why do you want to work here, and why now?

Candidate asks manager: "What are the top priorities for this role in the first month?"

Instructions: Start with a handshake and greeting. Ask all six questions. Ask at least one follow-up question. At the end, the candidate asks the manager question. Listen carefully. Keep each interview 6-8 minutes. Make eye contact. Do not write.
