1. The Six Meanings

Each meaning has one pattern.

Habit /
Fact

Present Simple
TIME WORDS:
always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day/week/month
  • I walk to work.
  • She drinks coffee every morning.
  • They don't eat meat.
  • He doesn't play soccer.
  • Do you speak Spanish?
  • Does she live here?

Finished
Action

Past Simple
TIME WORDS:
yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 2020, when I was young
  • I walked to the store yesterday.
  • She ate breakfast at 8:00.
  • They didn't watch the movie.
  • He didn't call his mom.
  • Did you finish the project?
  • Did she see the email?

Plans /
Promises

Future Simple
TIME WORDS:
tomorrow, next week/month/year, soon, later, in the future
  • I will help you tomorrow.
  • She will call you later.
  • They won't arrive on time.
  • He won't forget.
  • Will you come to the party?
  • Will she finish the report?

Now /
Near Future

Present Continuous
TIME WORDS:
now, right now, at the moment, currently, today, this week
  • I am studying right now.
  • She is cooking dinner.
  • They aren't watching TV.
  • He isn't working today.
  • Are you listening?
  • Is she coming tomorrow?

Interrupted
Background

Past Continuous
TIME WORDS:
when, while, at that moment, at 6:00 yesterday, all day/night
  • I was sleeping when you called.
  • She was reading a book.
  • They weren't paying attention.
  • He wasn't driving fast.
  • Were you waiting long?
  • Was she working at 9:00?

In Progress
Later

Future Continuous
TIME WORDS:
at 3:00 tomorrow, this time next week, when you arrive, all day tomorrow
  • I will be working at 3:00 PM.
  • She will be sleeping when you arrive.
  • They won't be traveling tomorrow.
  • He won't be studying then.
  • Will you be waiting for me?
  • Will she be using the car?

3. Simple vs. Continuous

What's the difference?

Present: Habit / Fact vs. Now

Simple (Habit)

Regular actions.
"I drink coffee every morning."

Continuous (Now)

Right now.
"I am drinking coffee right now."

1. I am talking on the phone right now.

2. I talk to my mom every Sunday.

Past: Finished vs. Background

Simple (Finished)

Specific time.
"I cooked dinner at 6:00."

Continuous (Background)

Already in progress.
"I was cooking dinner when you called."

1. I slept for 8 hours last night.

2. I was sleeping when the alarm rang.

Future: Fact vs. In Progress

Simple (Fact / Plan)

General future.
"I will work tomorrow."

Continuous (Progress)

Active at that time.
"I will be working at 10:00."

1. I will watch the movie later.

2. At 10 PM tonight, I will be watching a movie.

7. Correct Patterns

The Secret to Winning the Game

The Pattern Formula

WHO + VERB + WHAT + WHERE + WHEN

This is the "Golden Path" for every sentence!

Can we move the info?

Mostly, NO. You must keep WHO + VERB together.

Standard: They played soccer in the park last weekend.

Option: Last weekend, they played soccer in the park.
