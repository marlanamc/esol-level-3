Introducing the Past Participle - V3

The Past Participle (V3) is the third form of a verb. We use it with "have/has" or "had" to make perfect tenses.

Examples:
• Regular verb: talk → talked (V2) → talked (V3)
• Irregular verb: go → went (V2) → gone (V3)

Perfect Tense Formula Chart

Use these formulas to create perfect tenses:

Affirmative Negative Question
Present Perfect subject + have/has + V3 subject + have/has + not + V3 Have/Has + subject + V3
Past Perfect subject + had + V3 subject + had + not + V3 Had + subject + V3
Future Perfect subject + will + have + V3 subject + will + not + have + V3 Will + subject + have + V3
Important Note:
Most of the time, in regular verbs, V2 and V3 are the same!
But there are many irregular verbs we need to memorize!
