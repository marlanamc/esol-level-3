Comparing and Describing Amounts

"This is the cheapest apartment I found." "I have less time but more responsibilities."

Part 1: Superlatives

The Most, The Best, The -est

3

What Are Superlatives?

Superlatives identify the extreme in a group - the highest, lowest, best, or worst.

Use them when comparing three or more things.

Superlative (3+ things)

This is the cheapest apartment in the neighborhood.

Comparing this apartment to ALL others

Comparative (2 things)

This apartment is cheaper than that one.

Comparing only 2 apartments

4

How to Form Superlatives

Type Pattern Examples
Short adjectives (1 syllable) the + adjective + -est cheap ‚Üí the cheapest, old ‚Üí the oldest
Adjectives ending in -y the + adjective (y‚Üíiest) busy ‚Üí the busiest, happy ‚Üí the happiest
Long adjectives (2+ syllables) the most/least + adjective expensive ‚Üí the most expensive
Irregular adjectives (special forms) good ‚Üí the best, bad ‚Üí the worst
Common Mistake

Never say "the most cheap" - use "the cheapest" for short adjectives!

Part 2: Quantifiers

How Much? How Many?

7

Countable vs Uncountable Nouns

Before using quantifiers, you need to know if a noun is countable or uncountable.

üî¢ Countable Nouns

You can count them: 1, 2, 3...

  • jobs - 1 job, 2 jobs, 3 jobs
  • hours - 1 hour, 2 hours
  • bedrooms - 1 bedroom, 2 bedrooms
  • apartments - 1 apartment, 2 apartments

üíß Uncountable Nouns

You can't count them directly

  • money - NOT "1 money, 2 moneys"
  • time - NOT "1 time, 2 times"
  • experience - NOT "1 experience"
  • traffic - NOT "1 traffic"
Quick Test

Can you put a number in front of it? If yes, it's countable!

8

Many vs Much

This is one of the most common mistakes in English!

MANY = Countable   |   MUCH = Uncountable

‚úÖ MANY + Countable

How many hours do you work?
She has many responsibilities.

‚úÖ MUCH + Uncountable

I don't have much time.
This job requires much experience.
9

Common Mistakes with Many/Much

Wrong
How much hours do you work?
Correct
How many hours do you work?
Wrong
I don't have many time.
Correct
I don't have much time.

Few vs Little

Both mean "not enough" - but which noun are you describing?

FEW = Countable (small number)  |  LITTLE = Uncountable (small amount)

FEW + Countable

  • few options
  • few openings
  • few applicants
There are very few apartments available.

LITTLE + Uncountable

  • little money
  • little space
  • little hope
I have very little patience.
11

A Few vs Few / A Little vs Little

The word "a" changes the meaning!

A FEW / A LITTLE (Positive)

= some, enough for something

I have a few friends here.

= I do have some friends! (positive)

I have a little time.

= I have some time (enough)

FEW / LITTLE (Negative)

= almost none, not enough

I have few friends here.

= I'm lonely (negative)

I have little time.

= I'm rushed (not enough)

12

Fewer vs Less: The Grocery Store Rule

You've seen "10 items or less" at the supermarket? It's wrong!

It should be "10 items or fewer" because items are countable.

FEWER = Countable (comparing numbers)  |  LESS = Uncountable (comparing amounts)

FEWER + Countable

  • fewer hours
  • fewer employees
  • fewer benefits

LESS + Uncountable

  • less time
  • less stress
  • less money
My new job has fewer hours but less stress.

Most vs Least: The Extremes

Good news! Most and Least work with BOTH countable and uncountable nouns!

MOST = Maximum/Highest

  • This job has the most benefits.
  • Monday has the most traffic.
  • She does the most work.

LEAST = Minimum/Lowest

  • This option costs the least money.
  • Wednesday has the least traffic.
  • That job requires the least experience.

Works with both:

  • the most hours (countable) / the most time (uncountable)
  • the least problems (countable) / the least stress (uncountable)

Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

Quantifier Use With Example
many Countable many jobs, many hours
much Uncountable much time, much stress
few Countable (not enough) few options, few applicants
little Uncountable (not enough) little money, little hope
fewer Countable (comparison) fewer hours, fewer problems
less Uncountable (comparison) less time, less stress
most/least Both! the most hours, the most time

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Wrong
"much hours" ‚Üí many hours (hours are countable)
Wrong
"less employees" ‚Üí fewer employees (employees are countable)
Wrong
"the most cheap" ‚Üí the cheapest (short adjective = -est)
Wrong
"more better" ‚Üí better (better already means "more good")
The Ultimate Test

Can I say "one ___"? If yes ‚Üí countable (use many/few/fewer). If no ‚Üí uncountable (use much/little/less).
