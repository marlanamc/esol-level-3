What is Present Perfect? 2

Definition

The Present Perfect tense connects the past with the present.

We use it to talk about:

  • Actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past
  • Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
  • Experiences in our lives
  • Recent actions with present results
Formula 3
Subject + have/has + Past Participle (V3)
Remember: Use have with I, You, We, They.
Use has with He, She, It.

Examples:

â€¢ I have finished my homework.

â€¢ She has visited Paris three times.

When to Use Present Perfect 4

1. Unspecified Time

"I have visited Paris." (when doesn't matter)

2. Recent Actions

"She has just finished her homework."

3. Life Experiences

"I have never seen a whale."

4. Continuing Actions

"We have lived here for 5 years."

Common Time Words 5

These words often appear with Present Perfect:

already yet just ever never before so far recently lately for since
Important: We DON'T use Present Perfect with specific past times like "yesterday", "last week", "in 2020". Use Past Simple for those!
Present Perfect vs Past Simple 6

Main Rule:

Past Simple

Finished action at a SPECIFIC time in the past

Time words: yesterday, last week, in 2020, ago

Present Perfect

Action with connection to NOW (no specific time)

Time words: already, yet, just, ever, never, for, since

Examples:

Past: "I went to Paris last year."

Perfect: "I have been to Paris." (no specific time)

Quick Reference Guide 23

Use Past Simple When:

  • Specific time mentioned
  • yesterday, last week, in 2020
  • 3 days ago
  • Finished actions
  • Completed events

Use Present Perfect When:

  • No specific time
  • already, yet, just, ever, never
  • for + period, since + point
  • Life experiences
  • Recent/relevant actions
