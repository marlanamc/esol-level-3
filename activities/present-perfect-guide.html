Present Perfect Tense Guide

What is Present Perfect?

The Present Perfect tense connects the past with the present. We use it to talk about:

  • Actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past
  • Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
  • Experiences in our lives
  • Recent actions with present results

Formula

Subject + have/has + Past Participle (V3)
Remember: Use have with I, You, We, They. Use has with He, She, It.

When to Use Present Perfect

1. Unspecified Time: "I have visited Paris." (when doesn't matter) | 2. Recent Actions: "She has just finished her homework." | 3. Life Experiences: "I have never seen a whale." | 4. Continuing Actions: "We have lived here for 5 years."

Common Time Words with Present Perfect

These words often appear with Present Perfect:

already yet just ever never before so far recently lately for since
Important: We DON'T use Present Perfect with specific past times like "yesterday", "last week", "in 2020". Use Past Simple for those!
